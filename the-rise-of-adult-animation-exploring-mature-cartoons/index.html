The Rise of Adult Animation: Exploring Mature Cartoons

3 min · 635 words

As the world of animation continues to develop, we are seeing an incredible shift towards adult-oriented animation. Long gone are the days when cartoons were exclusively for kids. Today, many of the most compelling animated shows on TV are aimed squarely at adults. These programs explore complex topics and engage with mature themes, often pushing the boundaries of what is acceptable on mainstream TV. In this blog post, we will explore the rise of adult animation and delve into some of the most exciting, boundary-pushing cartoons around.

Firstly, let’s discuss why adult animation has risen in popularity. As our society continues to evolve, our tastes in entertainment have shifted. Audiences are no longer content with simplistic, child-friendly programming. We crave stories that are rich, nuanced, and complex. Adult animation provides a platform for precisely this kind of storytelling. The animation medium allows creators to depict anything, no matter how fantastical or otherworldly, without the constraints of live-action material. Animated shows can also tackle sensitive issues and complex themes, such as depression, addiction, and mental health, with a lighter, more approachable tone.

One of the most notable examples of a mature animated series is BoJack Horseman. The show centers around the titular BoJack, a washed-up horse actor struggling with mental health and addiction. BoJack’s story is dark and harrowing, but it is told in a format that’s accessible to viewers of all ages. The show is notable not only for its mature storytelling but also for its unique animation style. BoJack Horseman’s character designs and animation are whimsical and playful, juxtaposing the show’s weighty subject matter.

Another series that has helped usher in the era of adult animation is Rick and Morty. This show has amassed an enormous following over its four seasons, thanks in no small part to its daring, irreverent humor and its subversive take on sci-fi tropes. Like BoJack Horseman, Rick and Morty isn’t afraid to tackle serious issues while still maintaining its wacky, zany sensibility. In a world where most sci-fi shows are focused on spectacle and grandiosity, Rick and Morty instead opts for a more introspective approach.

Finally, we have The Venture Bros., a show that has been on the air for over a decade but remains beloved by audiences to this day. The Venture Bros. has always been a show for ’nerds’ and ‘geeks,’ with loads of Easter eggs and references to a wide variety of pop culture touchstones. However, it’s also a show that tackles complex themes like sexuality, gender identity, and relationships. The show’s diverse cast of characters allows for a wide range of stories, all told through the singular lens of its creator, Jackson Publick.

In conclusion, the rise of adult animation has been a long time coming. As our tastes in entertainment continue to shift, we crave shows that are mature and nuanced, yet still accessible and fun. With shows like BoJack Horseman, Rick and Morty, and The Venture Bros., we have a new breed of animated series that not only entertains but also challenges us to think about the world in new ways. Whether you’re a casual viewer or a hardcore fan of animation, these shows offer something for everyone. The future of adult animation is bright, and we can’t wait to see what comes next.
