5 Simple and Delicious Seafood Recipes to Try

3 min · 572 words

Seafood lovers rejoice! There’s nothing quite like the taste of fresh seafood to transport you to the coast with every bite. Whether you’re a seafood aficionado or just looking to incorporate more ocean-inspired dishes into your repertoire, these five simple and delicious seafood recipes are sure to tantalize your taste buds:

  1. Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp:

  2. Ingredients:

    • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

  3. Instructions:

    1. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
    2. Add shrimp to the skillet and season with salt and pepper. Cook until shrimp turn pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side.
    3. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the shrimp and sprinkle with chopped parsley. Serve immediately with your favorite side dishes.

  4. Grilled Salmon with Honey Mustard Glaze:

  5. Ingredients:

    • 4 salmon fillets
    • ¼ cup Dijon mustard
    • 2 tablespoons honey
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  6. Instructions:

    1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
    2. In a small bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to make the glaze.
    3. Brush salmon fillets with the honey mustard glaze on both sides.
    4. Grill salmon for 4-5 minutes per side, or until fish flakes easily with a fork. Serve hot with grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.

  7. Crispy Baked Cod with Herbed Panko Crust:

  8. Ingredients:

    • 4 cod fillets
    • 1 cup Panko breadcrumbs
    • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  9. Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    2. In a bowl, combine Panko breadcrumbs, chopped parsley, chopped dill, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
    3. Place cod fillets on the prepared baking sheet and pat dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper.
    4. Press the Panko mixture onto the top of each cod fillet to form a crust.
    5. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the crust is golden brown and crispy. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

  10. Garlic Butter Scallops:

  11. Ingredients: * 1 pound sea scallops * 4 tablespoons unsalted butter * 4 cloves garlic, minced * Salt and pepper to taste * Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

  12. Instructions:

1.  Pat scallops dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper.
2.  In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
3.  Add scallops to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and opaque.
4.  Sprinkle chopped parsley over the scallops and serve immediately with your favorite side dishes.

  1. Cajun Shrimp Pasta:

  2. Ingredients: * 8 ounces linguine pasta * 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined * 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning * 2 tablespoons olive oil * 4 cloves garlic, minced * 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved * 1 cup heavy cream * Salt and pepper to taste * Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

  3. Instructions: 1. Cook linguine pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. 2. In a bowl, toss shrimp with Cajun seasoning until evenly coated. 3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. 4. Add seasoned shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and opaque. 5. Stir in halved cherry tomatoes and cook for an additional 2 minutes. 6. Reduce heat to low and pour in heavy cream. Cook until sauce has thickened slightly, about 2-3 minutes. 7. Season sauce with salt and pepper to taste. Add cooked linguine to the skillet and toss to coat. 8. Serve Cajun shrimp pasta hot, garnished with chopped parsley.

These simple and delicious seafood recipes are perfect for weeknight dinners or special occasions. From succulent shrimp to flaky salmon, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So head to your kitchen and whip up one of these mouthwatering dishes tonight!
