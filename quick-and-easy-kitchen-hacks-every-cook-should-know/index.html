Quick and Easy Kitchen Hacks Every Cook Should Know

4 min · 687 words

Cooking is a common activity in almost every household. Whether you are a seasoned chef or a beginner, everyone could use some kitchen hacks to make cooking easier and more efficient. In this blog post, we will be exploring quick and easy kitchen hacks that every cook should know. These hacks will not only save you time and effort but also help you become a more organized and efficient cook.

1. Keep your cutting board in place

Do you often find your cutting board sliding around while you are chopping? A simple solution to this problem is to place a damp paper towel or a non-slip mat underneath your cutting board. This will keep it in place and prevent any accidents while you’re preparing your ingredients.

2. Use a spoon for peeling ginger

Peeling ginger can be a tedious task, but with this simple hack, you can save time and minimize waste. Instead of using a peeler or a knife, grab a spoon. Gently scrape the skin off the ginger using the edge of the spoon. This method allows you to remove the skin without removing too much of the ginger flesh.

3. Prevent your pots from boiling over

Boiling liquids can sometimes cause a mess in the kitchen, especially if you’re not keeping a close eye on the pot. To prevent your pots from boiling over, simply place a wooden spoon across the top of the pot. The spoon will break the surface tension and prevent the liquid from boiling over.

4. Make your own buttermilk substitute

Not having buttermilk on hand doesn’t mean you have to skip a recipe that calls for it. You can easily make your own buttermilk substitute by adding one tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to one cup of milk. Let it sit for a few minutes, and you’ll have a perfect substitute for buttermilk.

5. Freeze leftover herbs in olive oil

If you find yourself with leftover herbs that are about to go bad, don’t throw them away. Chop them up, place them in an ice cube tray, and cover them with olive oil. Freeze the tray, and you’ll have ready-to-use herb cubes whenever you need them. This not only prevents wastage but also adds flavor to your dishes.

6. Revive stale bread with water and an oven

Stale bread can be a disappointment, but you don’t have to throw it away. To revive it, simply wet your hands or a pastry brush and brush some water onto the surface of the bread. Place it in a preheated oven at 350°F for a few minutes, and it will come out fresh and delicious.

7. Cut tomatoes hassle-free

Cutting tomatoes can be a messy task, but there’s a simple hack to make it easier. Instead of using a regular knife, use a serrated knife to slice through the skin without crushing the flesh. Not only will this make the process hassle-free, but it will also result in cleaner and neater slices.

8. Easy eggshell removal

We’ve all had moments where a small piece of eggshell ends up in our bowl of cracked eggs. Instead of trying to fish it out with your fingers, use the larger piece of eggshell to scoop it out. The smaller piece of shell will be attracted to the larger one, making it easier to remove.

9. Store potatoes with apples

To prevent your potatoes from sprouting, store them with apples. Apples produce ethylene gas, which inhibits the sprouting process in potatoes. Keep a few apples in your potato storage bag or container, and they will help keep your potatoes fresh for longer.

10. Microwave citrus fruits for easier juicing

Before juicing citrus fruits like lemons or limes, microwave them for a few seconds. The heat will make the fruit softer and easier to juice. Simply roll the fruit on the countertop to break down the pulp, then cut and squeeze for maximum juice extraction.

Conclusion

Incorporating these quick and easy kitchen hacks into your cooking routine can save you time, effort, and frustration in the kitchen. From keeping your cutting board in place to reviving stale bread, these hacks will make your culinary journey smoother and more enjoyable. So, the next time you step into the kitchen, remember these kitchen hacks and impress yourself with your cooking skills. Happy cooking!
