Building a Survival Library: Essential Books for Preppers

3 min · 627 words

In today’s uncertain world, being prepared for any situation is becoming increasingly important. Whether you’re facing natural disasters, economic instability, or even a global pandemic, having the knowledge and skills to survive can make all the difference. One of the most valuable resources for any prepper is a well-curated survival library. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the essential books that every prepper should have in their collection.

  1. ‘SAS Survival Handbook’ by John ‘Lofty’ Wiseman: This timeless classic is often referred to as the ultimate guide to surviving any situation. Packed with practical advice on shelter, food, water, and navigation, this book is a must-have for anyone serious about preparedness.

  2. ‘Bushcraft 101: A Field Guide to the Art of Wilderness Survival’ by Dave Canterbury: If you’re looking to develop your wilderness survival skills, this is the book for you. From building shelters to making fire without matches, Dave Canterbury covers it all in this comprehensive guide.

  3. ‘The Prepper’s Blueprint: The Step-By-Step Guide To Help You Through Any Disaster’ by Tess Pennington: This book takes a holistic approach to preparedness, covering everything from food and water storage to emergency medical care. With detailed instructions and checklists, it’s an invaluable resource for preppers of all levels.

  4. ‘Emergency Food Storage & Survival Handbook’ by Peggy Layton: In times of crisis, having an adequate food supply is crucial. This book provides practical advice on how to build a long-term food storage plan, including what to store and how to rotate your supplies.

  5. ‘Where There Is No Doctor: A Village Health Care Handbook’ by David Werner: In a disaster scenario where medical help may not be available, this book could be a lifesaver. Covering everything from basic first aid to treating common illnesses and injuries, it’s an essential resource for anyone concerned about healthcare in survival situations.

  6. ‘The Encyclopedia of Country Living’ by Carla Emery: This comprehensive guide to self-sufficiency covers everything from gardening and raising livestock to preserving food and making your own household products. It’s a treasure trove of practical knowledge for anyone looking to live off the grid.

  7. ‘Nuclear War Survival Skills’ by Cresson H. Kearny: While the prospect of nuclear war may seem remote, it’s always better to be prepared. This book provides practical advice on how to survive a nuclear attack, from building fallout shelters to treating radiation exposure.

  8. ‘The Complete Guide to Hunting, Butchering, and Cooking Wild Game’ by Steven Rinella: In a survival scenario, knowing how to hunt and butcher wild game could mean the difference between life and death. Steven Rinella’s comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know, from tracking and stalking prey to preparing delicious meals in the wilderness.

  9. ‘The Ultimate Dehydrator Cookbook: The Complete Guide to Drying Food’ by Tammy Gangloff, Steven Gangloff, and September Ferguson: Dehydrating food is one of the most effective ways to preserve it for long-term storage. This book provides a wealth of recipes and techniques for dehydrating fruits, vegetables, meats, and more.

  10. ‘Deep Survival: Who Lives, Who Dies, and Why’ by Laurence Gonzales: Surviving a disaster isn’t just about having the right skills and knowledge—it’s also about maintaining the right mindset. Drawing on real-life stories of survival, this book explores the psychology of survival and offers valuable insights into what it takes to overcome adversity.

In conclusion, building a survival library is essential for anyone serious about preparedness. By investing in the right books and taking the time to educate yourself, you can ensure that you’re ready to face whatever challenges may come your way. Whether you’re a seasoned prepper or just getting started, these ten books are a great place to begin building your library.
