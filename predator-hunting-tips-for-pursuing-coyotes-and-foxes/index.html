Predator Hunting: Tips for Pursuing Coyotes and Foxes

3 min · 595 words

Predator hunting is an exhilarating and challenging pursuit that requires skill, patience, and knowledge of your prey. In this blog post, we will explore some essential tips and techniques for successfully hunting coyotes and foxes. From scouting to calling, we’ll cover everything you need to know to increase your chances of a successful hunt.

1. Scouting:
Before heading out into the field, it’s crucial to scout the area you plan to hunt. Look for signs of predator activity such as tracks, scat, and kill sites. Pay attention to the time of day when these signs are most prevalent, as this will give you an idea of when to focus your hunting efforts.

2. Choosing the Right Equipment:
The right equipment can make a significant difference in your hunting success. Opt for a high-quality rifle or shotgun that is accurate and powerful enough to take down coyotes and foxes. Consider using a variable magnification scope to increase your chances of making a precise shot. Additionally, invest in predator-specific calls, such as rabbit distress or coyote howls, to attract your target.

3. Concealment:
Predators like coyotes and foxes have keen senses, including sharp eyesight and a strong sense of smell. To increase your chances of a successful hunt, it’s crucial to blend in with your surroundings and minimize your scent. Wear camouflage clothing that matches the terrain you’ll be hunting in, and use scent-eliminating sprays to reduce human scent.

4. Master the Art of Calling:
Calling is an essential technique in predator hunting. Learn to mimic the distress sounds of small mammals such as rabbits or rodents to attract coyotes and foxes. Start with soft and subtle calls and gradually increase the volume and intensity to pique the curiosity of nearby predators. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time for predators to respond to your calls.

5. Set Up Properly:
When setting up for a predator hunt, it’s crucial to position yourself in a location that provides good visibility and shooting lanes. Choose a spot with a clear line of sight and make sure you have a solid rest for your rifle or shotgun. Consider using a portable blind or natural cover to further conceal your presence.

6. Hunt During the Golden Hours:
Predators are most active during the early morning and late evening hours, often referred to as the golden hours. Take advantage of this time to increase your chances of encountering coyotes and foxes. Set up well before sunrise or a few hours before sunset to maximize your hunting opportunities.

7. Practice Ethical Hunting:
As responsible hunters, it’s essential to practice ethical hunting techniques. Make sure you are familiar with local hunting regulations and follow them diligently. Always strive for clean and humane shots to minimize any unnecessary suffering. Additionally, consider donating any harvested animals to local fur harvesters or wildlife management agencies to help maintain a healthy predator population.

Predator hunting can be an exciting and rewarding experience for those willing to put in the time and effort. By following these tips and techniques, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a successful predator hunter. Remember to always prioritize safety, respect for wildlife, and conservation efforts. Happy hunting!
