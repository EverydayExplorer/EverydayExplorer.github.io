Wholesome and Delicious Buddha Bowl Recipes

3 min · 572 words

Buddha bowls have become increasingly popular in the past few years, and for good reason. These bowls are packed with nutrient-dense ingredients and are incredibly versatile. Not only are they visually appealing, but they’re also easy to make and can be customized to suit any dietary needs or preferences.

If you’re not already familiar with Buddha bowls, they typically consist of a variety of colorful vegetables, grains, and protein. The key to a great Buddha bowl is to choose a variety of ingredients that complement each other in flavor and texture. Here are some wholesome and delicious Buddha bowl recipes to add to your meal planner.

1. Thai Peanut Buddha Bowl

If you love Thai food, this Buddha bowl is perfect for you. Loaded with fresh vegetables, quinoa, and a tasty peanut sauce, this bowl is both satisfying and delicious.

Ingredients:
1 cup quinoa
2 cups water
1 red bell pepper, sliced
2 cups kale, chopped
1 cup shredded carrots
1/2 cup edamame
1/4 cup sliced green onion
1/4 cup roasted peanuts
1/4 cup chopped cilantro

For the peanut sauce:
1/2 cup peanut butter
2 tbsp soy sauce
1 tbsp honey
1 tbsp Sriracha
1 tsp garlic powder
1/4 cup warm water

Directions:
1. Cook quinoa according to instructions on the package.
2. In a large bowl, mix together the red pepper, kale, carrots, edamame, green onion, peanuts, and cilantro.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, Sriracha, garlic powder, and warm water to make the peanut sauce.
4. To assemble the Buddha bowl, add the cooked quinoa to the bowl of vegetables and top with the peanut sauce.

2. Mexican Buddha Bowl

This Buddha bowl is inspired by Mexican cuisine and is loaded with black beans, avocado, tomatoes, and spices.

Ingredients:
1 cup brown rice
2 cups water
1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
1 avocado, diced
1 cup chopped tomatoes
1/4 cup chopped cilantro
1/4 cup sliced green onion
1/2 tsp paprika
1/2 tsp cumin
Pinch of salt and pepper

For the dressing:
1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
1/4 cup salsa
1 tbsp lime juice
1/2 tsp garlic powder

Directions:
1. Cook brown rice according to instructions on package.
2. In a large bowl, mix together the black beans, avocado, tomatoes, cilantro, and green onion. Add the paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper and mix well.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, salsa, lime juice, and garlic powder to make the dressing.
4. To assemble the Buddha bowl, add the cooked brown rice to the vegetable mixture, and top with the dressing.

3. Mediterranean Buddha Bowl

This Buddha bowl is inspired by Mediterranean cuisine and is loaded with chickpeas, roasted vegetables, and a tangy lemon tahini dressing.

Ingredients:
1 cup quinoa
2 cups water
1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
1 red bell pepper, sliced
1 zucchini, sliced
1/4 cup sliced red onion
1/4 cup chopped parsley
1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese

For the dressing:
1/4 cup tahini
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 garlic clove, minced
1/4 tsp salt
1/4 cup warm water

Directions:
1. Cook quinoa according to instructions on package.
2. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place the bell pepper, zucchini, and onion on a baking sheet and roast for 15-20 minutes, or until tender.
3. In a large bowl, mix together the chickpeas, roasted vegetables, parsley, and feta.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and warm water to make the dressing.
5. To assemble the Buddha bowl, add the cooked quinoa to the vegetable mixture and top with the lemon tahini dressing.

Creating a Buddha bowl is simple and customizable to your preferences. These three wholesome and delicious recipes are just the start to your culinary exploration of the Buddha bowl. Remember to choose a variety of flavors, textures, and ingredients to create a bowl that is balanced and satisfying. Enjoy!
