The Best Hunting Calls for Big Game: Attract Deer and Elk

3 min · 595 words

Hunting big game, such as deer and elk, requires skill, patience, and the right tools. One essential tool that every hunter should have in their arsenal is a hunting call. A hunting call is a device that imitates the sounds made by animals in order to attract them. In this blog post, we will explore the best hunting calls for attracting deer and elk, helping you increase your chances of a successful hunt.

1. Grunt Calls:
One of the most effective hunting calls for attracting deer is the grunt call. The grunt call imitates the low, guttural sounds made by bucks during the rutting season. By using a grunt call, you can mimic the sound of a dominant buck, which can attract other bucks looking for a challenge or does looking for a mate. There are various types of grunt calls available, including adjustable calls that allow you to change the pitch and volume to match the specific situation.

2. Rattling Antlers:
Another effective hunting call for attracting deer, especially during the rut, is the rattling antlers. Rattling antlers simulate the sound of two bucks fighting, which can pique the curiosity of nearby deer. This call is particularly effective during the peak of the rut when bucks are actively seeking out potential mates and defending their territories. When using rattling antlers, it is important to create a realistic rattling sound by clashing the antlers together in a way that mimics a genuine fight.

3. Bugle Calls:
For hunting elk, bugle calls are a must-have. Elk bugling is a unique vocalization made by bull elk during the rut. By using a bugle call, hunters can imitate this distinct sound and attract bulls. The bugle call is especially effective when hunting during the early morning or late evening when elk are most active. It is important to practice using a bugle call to ensure that you can produce a realistic bugling sound that will grab the attention of nearby elk.

4. Cow Calls:
In addition to bugle calls, cow calls are also effective for hunting elk. Cow calls imitate the sounds made by female elk, which can attract bulls looking for a mate. Cow calls come in various forms, including mouth calls, reed calls, and electronic calls. It is important to choose a cow call that suits your hunting style and practice using it to produce accurate and convincing cow sounds.

5. Electronic Calls:
For both deer and elk hunting, electronic calls can be a game-changer. Electronic calls are pre-recorded or programmable devices that produce realistic animal sounds at the push of a button. These calls can imitate a wide range of sounds, including grunts, bugles, and cow calls, making them versatile and effective tools for attracting big game. When using electronic calls, it is important to familiarize yourself with the specific sounds and behaviors of the animals you are targeting to ensure that you are using the right call at the right time.

In conclusion, having the right hunting calls can significantly increase your chances of attracting deer and elk during your hunts. Whether you choose to use grunt calls, rattling antlers, bugle calls, cow calls, or electronic calls, it is important to practice using these tools to produce realistic animal sounds. By mastering the art of calling, you can enhance your hunting experience and improve your chances of a successful hunt. So, gear up, practice your calls, and get ready for an exciting big game hunting season!
