Gin Guide: From London Dry to New World Styles

4 min · 642 words

Gin, the beloved spirit that has been a staple in bars and homes for centuries, has undergone a remarkable transformation over the years. From its origins in London to the emergence of new world styles, gin has captivated the taste buds of enthusiasts around the globe. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you on a journey through the fascinating world of gin, exploring its history, production methods, and the various styles that have emerged over time.

To fully appreciate the complexity and diversity of gin, it’s important to understand its origins. The story begins in the 17th century, when Dutch physician Franciscus Sylvius developed a medicinal juniper-based spirit known as ‘genever.’ It was believed to have therapeutic properties and was widely consumed for its alleged health benefits. However, it wasn’t until the early 18th century that gin truly took off in London.

London Dry Gin, the most well-known and traditional style of gin, emerged during this period. It is characterized by its juniper-forward flavor profile, with other botanicals playing a supporting role. London Dry Gin is known for its dryness, crispness, and versatility. It is often enjoyed in classic cocktails such as the Martini and the Negroni.

As the popularity of gin grew, so did the demand for new and exciting flavors. This led to the development of New World styles of gin, which deviate from the strict production methods of London Dry Gin. These styles embrace innovation and experimentation, pushing the boundaries of what gin can be.

One such style is the ‘Contemporary’ or ‘New Western’ gin, which places emphasis on the use of non-traditional botanicals. These gins often feature citrus, floral, or herbal notes, resulting in a more complex and aromatic spirit. Examples of contemporary gins include Hendrick’s and Monkey 47.

Another emerging style is the ‘Barrel-Aged’ or ‘Aged’ gin, which sees the spirit matured in oak barrels, similar to whiskey. This process imparts additional flavors and complexities, resulting in a smoother and richer gin. Barrel-aged gins are often enjoyed neat or in cocktails that benefit from their unique flavor profile.

The rise of craft distilleries has also contributed to the diversification of gin styles. Small-batch and artisanal gins have gained popularity, with distillers experimenting with unique botanical combinations and production techniques. These gins offer a truly bespoke drinking experience and are often sought after by gin connoisseurs.

When it comes to choosing a gin, there are no hard and fast rules. It ultimately comes down to personal preference and the occasion. Whether you enjoy the classic elegance of a London Dry Gin or the boldness of a New World style, there is a gin out there for everyone.

In conclusion, the world of gin is a vibrant and ever-evolving one. From the traditional London Dry Gin to the innovative New World styles, gin offers a wide range of flavors and experiences. So next time you find yourself at a bar or perusing the spirits aisle, take a moment to explore the diverse world of gin and discover your new favorite expression. Cheers!
