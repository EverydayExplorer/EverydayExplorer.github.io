The ExoMars Mission: Searching for Signs of Life on the Red Planet

4 min · 671 words

Humans have long been fascinated by the possibility of life on other planets, and Mars, our closest neighbor in the solar system, has always held a special place in our imaginations. The ExoMars mission, a joint endeavor by the European Space Agency (ESA) and the Russian space agency Roscosmos, is an ambitious project aimed at unraveling the mysteries of the Red Planet and searching for signs of life.

In this blog post, we will delve into the details of the ExoMars mission, its objectives, and the innovative scientific instruments that will help scientists determine whether Mars could have sustained or currently supports microbial life.

The ExoMars Mission

The ExoMars mission consists of two phases, each with its own set of objectives. The first phase, which was launched in 2016, involved the Trace Gas Orbiter (TGO) and the Schiaparelli lander. The TGO’s primary goal is to study the Martian atmosphere and search for trace gases, particularly methane, which could indicate the presence of biological or geological activity. The Schiaparelli lander, unfortunately, crash-landed on the planet’s surface and was unable to fulfill its role as a technology demonstrator for future Mars landings.

The second phase of the ExoMars mission, slated for launch between 2022 and 2023, is even more exciting. This phase includes the Rosalind Franklin rover, named after the renowned scientist who played a crucial role in the discovery of the structure of DNA. Equipped with a suite of state-of-the-art instruments, the rover will conduct detailed scientific analyses and drilling for samples up to two meters beneath the Martian surface.

Scientific Instruments on Board

The Rosalind Franklin rover is armed with a suite of scientific instruments designed to detect and examine signs of past or present life on Mars. One such instrument is the Mars Organic Molecule Analyzer (MOMA), which will analyze samples for organic compounds, the building blocks of life as we know it. MOMA will be able to identify complex molecules and study their composition, providing crucial insights into the planet’s potential for hosting life.

Another instrument on board is the Raman Laser Spectrometer (RLS). RLS uses lasers to identify and analyze the mineralogy of Martian rocks and soil. By examining the composition of these samples, scientists hope to gain a better understanding of the planet’s geological history and potential habitability.

Additionally, the Mars Organic Detector (MOD) will be used to search for specific organic molecules that could be associated with life. MOD will analyze samples collected by the rover’s drill and identify any signs of past or present organic compounds, providing valuable data on the potential for life on Mars.

The Quest for Life

One of the most intriguing objectives of the ExoMars mission is to search for signs of life, past or present, on the Red Planet. The presence of methane in the Martian atmosphere has intrigued scientists for years, as it could potentially point towards the existence of microbial life. Methane can be produced by both biological and geological processes, and understanding its origin on Mars could hold the key to unlocking the planet’s mysteries.

Another crucial aspect of the mission is the search for biosignatures within the Martian soil. By drilling beneath the surface, the Rosalind Franklin rover will collect samples that may contain preserved traces of ancient Martian life. The analysis of these samples will provide vital clues about the planet’s history and the possibility of past habitability.

Conclusion

The ExoMars mission represents a significant step forward in our quest to uncover the secrets of the Red Planet. With its advanced suite of scientific instruments and ambitious objectives, the mission aims to answer one of humanity’s most profound questions: are we alone in the universe?

As we eagerly await the second phase of the mission and the arrival of the Rosalind Franklin rover on Mars, scientists and space enthusiasts alike are filled with anticipation. The ExoMars mission has the potential to revolutionize our understanding of Mars and pave the way for future human exploration. Whether it uncovers signs of past or present life or provides us with new insights into the planet’s potential habitability, one thing is for certain: the ExoMars mission will undoubtedly go down in history as a significant milestone in our exploration of the cosmos.
