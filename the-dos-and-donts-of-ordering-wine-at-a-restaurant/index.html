The Dos and Don'ts of Ordering Wine at a Restaurant

4 min · 792 words

Walking into a restaurant and being handed the wine list can be an exciting moment for many people. However, it can also be a source of stress and anxiety for those who are not familiar with the ins and outs of ordering wine. Whether you are a wine connoisseur or a casual drinker, knowing the dos and don’ts of ordering wine at a restaurant can help you navigate the process with confidence and ease.

Dos:

1. Do ask for recommendations: If you are unsure about which wine to choose, don’t be afraid to ask the sommelier or server for recommendations. They are trained to help you find a wine that suits your taste preferences and budget. Be honest about your likes and dislikes, and they will be able to steer you in the right direction.

2. Do consider the setting: The type of wine you choose should complement the meal you are having and the occasion. If you are dining at a fancy restaurant for a special occasion, opt for a more upscale wine. If you are having a casual meal with friends, a more affordable bottle will do just fine.

3. Do know your budget: Before perusing the wine list, it’s a good idea to have an idea of how much you are willing to spend. This will help narrow down your choices and prevent any sticker shock when the bill arrives.

4. Do pair your wine with your food: Different wines pair better with certain types of food. If you are having seafood, a crisp white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay would be a good choice. For red meat, opt for a bold red like Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec. Don’t hesitate to ask your server for recommendations if you’re unsure.

5. Do check the wine list for unfamiliar wines: Restaurants often have a selection of unique and lesser-known wines that you may not find at your local liquor store. Take this opportunity to try something new and expand your palate.

Don’ts:

1. Don’t be afraid to ask questions: If you are unsure about anything on the wine list, don’t hesitate to ask for clarification. Whether it’s about a particular wine’s flavor profile, region, or vintage, the staff is there to help you make an informed decision.

2. Don’t choose wine based solely on price: While it’s important to stick to your budget, don’t let price be the sole determining factor in your wine selection. Some of the best wines can be found at reasonable prices, so don’t be afraid to explore different options.

3. Don’t dismiss the sommelier’s recommendations: Sommeliers are trained professionals who have a wealth of knowledge about wine. Trust their expertise and be open to trying something new based on their suggestions.

4. Don’t be swayed by peer pressure: Just because your dining companions are ordering a certain wine, it doesn’t mean you have to follow suit. Choose a wine that you will enjoy, regardless of what others at the table are having.

5. Don’t rush your decision: Take your time perusing the wine list and don’t feel pressured to make a quick decision. If you need more time to decide, ask the server for recommendations or a few more minutes to make your choice.

Ordering wine at a restaurant can be a fun and enjoyable experience when you know the dos and don’ts. By following these tips, you can confidently select a wine that complements your meal and enhances your dining experience. Cheers to good wine and good company!
