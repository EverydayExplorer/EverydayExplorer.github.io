The Best Fashion Movies for a Cozy Night In

3 min Â· 566 words

Fashion and film have long been intertwined, creating a glamorous and captivating synergy that delights audiences worldwide. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast, a movie buff, or simply someone seeking inspiration for your next cozy night in, there’s nothing quite like curling up with a stylish cinematic masterpiece. From iconic costume designs to compelling narratives set against the backdrop of the fashion world, these movies offer a perfect blend of entertainment and sartorial splendor. So, grab your popcorn, dim the lights, and prepare to be transported into the dazzling realm of fashion with our curated list of the best fashion movies for a cozy night in.

  1. The Devil Wears Prada (2006): Based on Lauren Weisberger’s bestselling novel, this film follows the journey of Andy Sachs, a young aspiring journalist who lands a job as the assistant to Miranda Priestly, the formidable editor-in-chief of the fashion magazine Runway. With its iconic fashion moments and stellar performances by Meryl Streep and Anne Hathaway, ‘The Devil Wears Prada’ offers a glimpse into the cutthroat world of high fashion.

  2. Funny Face (1957): This classic musical romantic comedy stars Audrey Hepburn as Jo Stockton, a shy bookstore clerk who is discovered by a fashion photographer and whisked away to Paris to become a model. With its dazzling costumes, catchy musical numbers, and timeless charm, ‘Funny Face’ is a delightful homage to the world of haute couture.

  3. Coco Before Chanel (2009): Directed by Anne Fontaine, this biographical drama chronicles the early life of legendary fashion designer Coco Chanel, from her humble beginnings as a seamstress to her rise to prominence in the world of fashion. Featuring stunning period costumes and a captivating performance by Audrey Tautou in the title role, ‘Coco Before Chanel’ offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a fashion icon.

  4. Phantom Thread (2017): Directed by Paul Thomas Anderson, this psychological drama stars Daniel Day-Lewis as Reynolds Woodcock, a renowned dressmaker in 1950s London whose meticulously crafted garments are sought after by royalty, celebrities, and socialites. When he meets Alma, a young woman who becomes his muse and lover, their relationship takes a dark and twisted turn. With its sumptuous cinematography and exquisite costume design, ‘Phantom Thread’ is a mesmerizing exploration of love, obsession, and the creative process.

  5. The September Issue (2009): This documentary offers a behind-the-scenes look at the making of the September 2007 issue of Vogue magazine, famously the largest and most important issue of the year. Directed by R.J. Cutler, the film provides a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of the fashion industry, showcasing the talent, dedication, and creativity that go into producing a fashion bible like Vogue.

  6. Yves Saint Laurent (2014): Directed by Jalil Lespert, this biographical drama chronicles the life and career of legendary fashion designer Yves Saint Laurent, from his early days at the House of Dior to his groundbreaking work as the co-founder of his own fashion label. Featuring stunning period costumes and a standout performance by Pierre Niney in the title role, ‘Yves Saint Laurent’ is a stylish and poignant tribute to one of the greatest fashion icons of the 20th century.

  7. The Great Gatsby (2013): While not strictly a fashion movie, Baz Luhrmann’s adaptation of F. Scott Fitzgerald’s classic novel is a visual feast for the senses, with its opulent costumes and lavish set design capturing the glamour and excess of the Roaring Twenties. From Daisy Buchanan’s ethereal dresses to Jay Gatsby’s dapper suits, ‘The Great Gatsby’ is a breathtaking ode to the Jazz Age and all its sartorial splendor.

  8. Funny Girl (1968): This musical biopic stars Barbra Streisand as Fanny Brice, a talented comedienne and singer who rises to fame in the early 20th century. While not strictly a fashion film, ‘Funny Girl’ features some iconic costumes designed by legendary Hollywood costume designer Irene Sharaff, including Brice’s signature black-and-white striped dress and feathered headpiece.

  9. The Dressmaker (2015): Directed by Jocelyn Moorhouse, this dark comedy-drama stars Kate Winslet as Tilly Dunnage, a skilled dressmaker who returns to her hometown in rural Australia to seek revenge on those who wronged her in the past. With its stunning costumes and sly wit, ‘The Dressmaker’ is a stylish and subversive take on the revenge thriller genre.

  10. Zoolander (2001): This cult classic comedy directed by Ben Stiller follows the misadventures of Derek Zoolander, a dim-witted male model who becomes embroiled in a plot to assassinate the Prime Minister of Malaysia. With its hilarious satire of the fashion industry and memorable cameos from fashion luminaries like Anna Wintour and Tommy Hilfiger, ‘Zoolander’ is a must-watch for fashion enthusiasts with a sense of humor.

Whether you’re in the mood for a glamorous Hollywood spectacle or a thought-provoking indie drama, these fashion movies are sure to delight and inspire. So, pour yourself a glass of wine, slip into your comfiest pajamas, and lose yourself in the captivating world of fashion from the comfort of your own home.
