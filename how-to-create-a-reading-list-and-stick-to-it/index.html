How to Create a Reading List and Stick to It

3 min · 612 words

Creating a reading list is a fantastic way to stay organized and keep track of the books you want to read. It’s easy to get overwhelmed with the endless choices of new releases, classic novels, and must-reads recommended by friends. Having a reading list can help you prioritize your reading and ensure you don’t miss out on books that pique your interest.

In this blog post, we will dive into the how-tos of creating a reading list and, most importantly, how to stick to it. We will discuss various approaches to curating your list, tips to manage your time effectively, and strategies that will help you stay motivated. So, without further ado, let’s get started!

Curating Your Reading List

  1. Identify your reading preferences: Consider the genres, authors, or topics that you want to explore. Think about what type of books excite you and make a note of them. This will form the foundation of your reading list.

  2. Utilize multiple sources: Browse bookstores, read book reviews, and utilize online platforms like Goodreads to discover new books. Joining book clubs or online reading communities can also expose you to diverse recommendations and help you find hidden gems.

  3. Prioritize your selections: Once you have a wide range of options, prioritize the books that stand out to you the most. Consider factors such as their relevance to your interests, the impact they have made, or if they tie into current events or topics of conversation.

  4. Maintain a balance: It’s important to maintain a balance between various genres and themes in your reading list. Add some non-fiction, fiction, classics, contemporary works, and even books from different cultures or time periods. This will ensure a diverse reading experience and expand your horizons.

Sticking to Your Reading List

  1. Set realistic goals: When creating your reading list, set realistic goals for yourself. Consider factors such as your reading speed, available time, and other commitments. Be honest with yourself about how many books you can realistically finish in a week, month, or year.

  2. Create a schedule: Treat your reading time as sacred and incorporate it into your daily or weekly routine. Whether it’s reading before bed, during your lunch break, or on weekends, creating a schedule will help you make reading a habit and stick to your list.

  3. Hold yourself accountable: Share your reading list with friends or join book clubs where you can discuss your progress and exchange recommendations. Having others hold you accountable can provide the motivation you need to stay on track.

  4. Avoid distractions: In today’s digital age, it’s easy to get distracted by social media or online streaming platforms. Set boundaries for yourself by scheduling dedicated reading time away from electronic distractions. Consider using reading apps that block or limit your access to certain websites or apps while you read.

  5. Celebrate small victories: Every time you finish a book on your reading list, celebrate! Rewarding yourself for completing a book can motivate you to continue reading and sticking to your list.

  6. Revisit and revise: Regularly review your reading list and make adjustments as needed. Your interests may change over time, and it’s essential to ensure your reading list continues to reflect your evolving preferences.

Conclusion

Creating a reading list is a powerful tool for any book lover. It allows you to prioritize your reading choices, explore new authors and genres, and ultimately stay focused on your reading goals. By following the tips outlined in this blog post, you will be well on your way to creating a reading list that is both manageable and enjoyable. So, start exploring, curating, and sticking to your reading list today! Happy reading!
