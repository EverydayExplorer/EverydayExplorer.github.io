The Wines of Greece: Ancient Varieties Rediscovered

4 min · 792 words

Of all the ancient civilizations that have left their mark on the world, Greece stands out as one of the most influential. From literature and philosophy to architecture and art, the Greeks have contributed greatly to our understanding of the world. However, there is one aspect of Greek culture that often goes unnoticed – their incredible wines.

For thousands of years, the Greeks have been producing some of the finest wines in the world. From the sun-drenched vineyards of Santorini to the lush valleys of Macedonia, Greece offers a wide variety of unique and delicious wines that are just waiting to be discovered.

One of the most fascinating aspects of Greek wines is the ancient grape varieties that they are made from. Greece is home to over 300 native grape varieties, many of which have been cultivated for thousands of years. These ancient varieties offer a glimpse into the illustrious history of Greek winemaking and provide a truly unique tasting experience.

One such grape variety is Assyrtiko, which is primarily grown on the island of Santorini. It is believed to be one of the oldest grape varieties in existence, dating back to ancient times. Assyrtiko wines are known for their vibrant acidity and mineral-rich flavors, reflecting the volcanic soils of Santorini. They pair exceptionally well with seafood and are often described as being crisp and refreshing.

Another ancient variety that has gained international recognition in recent years is Xinomavro. This red grape variety is grown primarily in northern Greece and produces wines that are robust, complex, and age-worthy. Xinomavro wines often exhibit flavors of red berries, tomatoes, and olives, making them a perfect match for Mediterranean cuisine.

In addition to these ancient varieties, Greece also produces wines from a number of other exciting grapes. Moschofilero, a pink-skinned grape, is grown in the Peloponnese region and produces aromatic and floral wines. Agiorgitiko, on the other hand, creates rich and full-bodied red wines that are often compared to those from Bordeaux.

While Greek wines may be ancient in their origins, the winemaking techniques used today have modernized significantly. The Greek wine industry has undergone a renaissance in recent years, with innovative winemakers combining tradition with new technology to produce wines of exceptional quality.

One example of this can be seen in the production of retsina, a traditional Greek wine that has been infused with resin from pine trees. Retsina has a long and storied history in Greece, but it was often dismissed as a low-quality wine. However, in recent years, winemakers have embraced modern techniques to produce high-quality retsina that is now gaining recognition on the international stage.

In terms of climate and terroir, Greece offers a diverse range of growing conditions. The warm Mediterranean climate provides ideal conditions for grape cultivation, with hot, dry summers and mild winters. The country’s varied topography, which includes mountains, valleys, and coastal plains, further contributes to the unique character of Greek wines.

Despite the many challenges faced by the Greek wine industry, such as economic instability and limited recognition in international markets, Greek wines are finally starting to gain the recognition they deserve. In recent years, Greek wines have been shining on the global stage, with prestigious awards and glowing reviews from wine critics.

If you’re a wine lover looking to explore new and exciting flavors, I highly recommend delving into the wines of Greece. Whether it’s a crisp Assyrtiko, a complex Xinomavro, or a fragrant Moschofilero, Greek wines offer a world of flavors just waiting to be discovered.

As you embark on your Greek wine journey, don’t forget to pair these wines with the incredible cuisine that Greece has to offer. From fresh seafood to indulgent moussaka, Greek cuisine is a perfect match for the vibrant and flavorsome wines produced in the country.

So next time you’re browsing the aisles of your local wine shop, be sure to seek out the wines of Greece. You may just uncover a hidden gem that transports you back to ancient times and leave you with a newfound appreciation for the rich history and excellent quality of Greek wines.
