The Dos and Don'ts of Open House Etiquette

3 min · 566 words

So, you’ve found the perfect open house to attend. Whether you’re a first-time homebuyer or a seasoned real estate enthusiast, it’s important to understand the dos and don’ts of open house etiquette. Open houses are not just an opportunity to view a potential new home; they are also a chance to make a positive impression on the seller and their real estate agent. By following proper etiquette, you can ensure a pleasant and productive experience for everyone involved. Here are some essential dos and don’ts to keep in mind when attending an open house.

The Dos

1. Do Arrive on Time

Punctuality is key when it comes to open houses. Arriving on time shows respect for the seller’s and the agent’s schedule. It also allows you to have ample time to explore the property without feeling rushed.

2. Do Introduce Yourself

When you arrive, take a moment to introduce yourself to the real estate agent hosting the open house. Providing your name and contact information shows that you are a serious and courteous potential buyer.

3. Do Ask Permission Before Taking Photos

It’s natural to want to capture the details of a property you’re interested in, but always ask for permission before taking any photos. Some sellers may have personal items on display that they prefer not to be photographed.

4. Do Come Prepared with Questions

Prepare a list of questions about the property and the neighborhood in advance. Asking thoughtful questions demonstrates your genuine interest and can provide you with valuable insights.

5. Do Respect the Property

Treat the open house as you would a private showing. Avoid touching personal belongings, and be mindful of the seller’s belongings throughout the home.

6. Do Provide Feedback

After the open house, consider providing constructive feedback to the real estate agent. This can help the seller understand how their property is being perceived by potential buyers.

7. Do Follow Up

If you’re interested in the property, follow up with the real estate agent after the open house to express your interest and ask any additional questions you may have.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Bring Uninvited Guests

Unless specifically stated, open houses are generally intended for potential buyers and their real estate agents. Avoid bringing along friends or family members who are not involved in the homebuying process.

2. Don’t Overstay Your Welcome

While it’s important to take your time exploring the property, be mindful of the open house hours. Avoid overstaying your welcome, as the seller and the real estate agent may have other appointments scheduled.

3. Don’t Be Disrespectful

It should go without saying, but always treat the property with respect. Avoid making negative comments about the home or the seller’s personal taste, as this can create an uncomfortable atmosphere for everyone.

4. Don’t Pry into Personal Belongings

Resist the temptation to snoop through the seller’s personal belongings. Remember that you are a guest in someone else’s home, and respect their privacy.

5. Don’t Skip Sign-In Procedures

Many open houses require visitors to sign in upon arrival. This is not only a security measure but also a way for the seller and real estate agent to keep track of who has visited the property.

6. Don’t Disregard COVID-19 Guidelines

In the current climate, it’s important to adhere to any COVID-19 guidelines set forth by the seller or the real estate agent. This may include wearing a mask, using hand sanitizer, or maintaining social distancing.

7. Don’t Hesitate to Ask Questions

While it’s important to be respectful of the seller’s property, don’t hesitate to ask the real estate agent any questions you may have. They are there to provide you with information and guidance.

By following these dos and don’ts of open house etiquette, you can make the most of your open house visit while leaving a positive impression. Remember that open houses are not only about finding the right property; they are also an opportunity to showcase your professionalism and courtesy as a potential buyer. Happy house hunting!
