The Art of Trolling: Techniques for Catching Fish on the Move

5 min · 961 words

Trolling is a tried and tested technique for catching fish on the move. Whether you’re fishing in saltwater or freshwater, trolling can be an effective way to catch a variety of species. In this article, we’ll look at some of the techniques and tips for successful trolling.

What is Trolling?

Trolling is a fishing method where one or more lines are trailed behind a moving boat. The boat can be powered by an engine or moved by paddling. The objective of trolling is to catch fish that are on the move, without having to chase after them. Trolling can be used to catch trout, salmon, walleye, bass, marlin, tuna, and many other species.

Trolling Techniques

The right technique can make all the difference when it comes to trolling. Here are some tips to keep in mind.

1. Use the Right Lure

Choosing the right lure is crucial for successful trolling. The lure you select will depend on the species you’re targeting, the waters you’re fishing in and the type of trolling you’re doing. Some of the most popular lures used for trolling including spoons, plugs, and spinners.

2. Maintain the Right Speed

The speed at which you’re trolling is also important. Not all fish swim at the same speed, so it’s important to adjust the speed according to the species you’re targeting. Generally, a speed of 2-4 miles per hour is ideal for most species.

3. Vary Your Lure Depth

Fish are not always found at the same depth. By varying the depth at which your lure is presented, you can increase your chances of catching fish. You can adjust your lure depth in a couple of ways. The first is to change the length of the line between the lure and the boat. The second is to use a downrigger, which will take your lure to a specific depth.

4. Use a Fish Finder

A fish finder is a useful tool that can help you locate fish when trolling. A fish finder uses sonar to detect fish and displays the information on a screen. By using a fish finder, you can identify the depth at which fish are swimming, helping you to adjust your trolling technique.

5. Pay Attention to the Environment

The environment in which you’re fishing will also play a role in the success of your trolling. Pay attention to weather conditions, water temperature, and other environmental factors that might affect fish behavior. For example, fish are more likely to be feeding in the early morning or late evening when the water is cooler.

Conclusion

Trolling is a versatile and effective fishing technique that can be used to catch many species of fish. By using the right techniques, you can increase your chances of success when trolling. The key is to choose the right lure, maintain the right speed, vary your lure depth, and pay attention to the environment. With these tips in mind, you can become a successful troll fisherman.
