The Best Fashion Documentaries to Inspire Your Style

3 min · 566 words

Fashion is not only about the latest trends and runway shows; it is a form of self-expression, a way to showcase our unique personalities through clothing and accessories. If you’re someone who is passionate about fashion and looking for some inspiration to take your style to the next level, then fashion documentaries are a great resource to explore. These documentaries not only provide an inside look into the fashion industry but also offer valuable insight and inspiration that can help you refine and redefine your personal style. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best fashion documentaries that are sure to inspire your style.

1. ‘The September Issue’ (2009): Directed by R.J. Cutler, this documentary gives viewers a peek into the world of Vogue magazine and the making of their iconic September issue. From the editorial meetings to the high-pressure fashion shoots, this film offers a rare behind-the-scenes look at the fashion industry’s most influential publication. With appearances from fashion icons like Anna Wintour and Grace Coddington, ‘The September Issue’ is a must-watch for any fashion enthusiast.

2. ‘Dior and I’ (2014): Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, this documentary takes you behind the scenes of the iconic fashion house Christian Dior. The film follows the journey of Raf Simons, the newly appointed artistic director, as he prepares his first haute couture collection. Witness the creative process, the meticulous craftsmanship, and the dedication of the team as they bring Simons’ vision to life. ‘Dior and I’ is a captivating exploration of the intersection of art, fashion, and creativity.

3. ‘Bill Cunningham New York’ (2010): Directed by Richard Press, this documentary pays homage to the late Bill Cunningham, a legendary street style photographer for The New York Times. Known for his keen eye and unwavering dedication to capturing fashion on the streets, Cunningham’s work is an inspiration to fashion lovers worldwide. The film delves into his unique approach to documenting style, his humble lifestyle, and his unwavering passion for fashion.

4. ‘The First Monday in May’ (2016): Directed by Andrew Rossi, this documentary takes you behind the scenes of the annual Met Gala, one of the most high-profile fashion events of the year. Explore the intricate planning and preparation that goes into curating the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s Costume Institute exhibition and the star-studded gala that follows. With appearances from fashion icons like Anna Wintour, Karl Lagerfeld, and Rihanna, ‘The First Monday in May’ offers a fascinating glimpse into the intersection of fashion, art, and celebrity.

5. ‘IRIS’ (2014): Directed by Albert Maysles, this documentary celebrates the life and style of Iris Apfel, an iconic fashion figure known for her bold and eclectic fashion choices. With her signature oversized glasses and vibrant ensembles, Apfel has become a fashion icon in her own right. This film explores her unique approach to style, her extensive collection of accessories, and her unapologetic self-expression through fashion.

These fashion documentaries offer a wealth of inspiration for anyone looking to elevate their personal style. From the glamorous world of Vogue to the streets of New York, these films provide a glimpse into the diverse and ever-evolving world of fashion. Whether you’re seeking creative ideas, a deeper understanding of the fashion industry, or simply some fashion-forward entertainment, these documentaries are sure to inspire your style and ignite your passion for fashion.

So, grab some popcorn, get cozy, and prepare to be inspired by the best fashion documentaries out there. Who knows, they might just be the catalyst for your next fashion-forward adventure!
