Types of Noise-Canceling Headphones

There are two main types of noise-canceling headphones: active noise-canceling (ANC) and passive noise-canceling (PNC).

Active noise-canceling headphones use microphones to listen for ambient noise and then create a sound wave that is equal in amplitude but opposite in phase to the noise. This effectively cancels out the noise, leaving you with a quieter listening experience.

Passive noise-canceling headphones use physical barriers to block out noise. This can be done through the use of ear cups that fit snugly around your ears, or through the use of earplugs that insert into your ear canal.

Features to Look for in Noise-Canceling Headphones

When choosing noise-canceling headphones, there are a few key features you should look for:

  • Noise cancellation performance: This is the most important factor to consider when choosing noise-canceling headphones. Make sure to read reviews from other users to get an idea of how well the headphones perform in different environments.
  • Comfort: You’ll be wearing your headphones for hours at a time, so it’s important to make sure they’re comfortable. Look for headphones with adjustable ear cups and a headband that doesn’t put too much pressure on your head.
  • Sound quality: Noise-canceling headphones should provide excellent sound quality, so you can enjoy your music or movies without any distractions. Look for headphones with a wide frequency response and clear, balanced sound.
  • Battery life: If you’re going to be using your headphones for long periods of time, you’ll need to make sure they have a long battery life. Look for headphones that have a battery life of at least 20 hours.
  • Features: Some noise-canceling headphones come with additional features, such as Bluetooth connectivity, voice assistant support, and touch controls. Consider which features are important to you when choosing headphones.
