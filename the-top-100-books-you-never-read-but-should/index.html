The Top 100 Books You Never Read But Should

4 min · 841 words

In today’s world, there is an abundance of books, and it can be challenging to sift through the countless titles to find the ones that truly deserve your time and attention. While some books are universally acclaimed and widely read, there are many hidden gems that often fly under the radar. Here, we present a list of 100 books that you may have never read but should. These books span various genres, time periods, and authors, but they all share one common trait: they are exceptional works of literature that deserve your attention.

1. The Shadow of the Wind by Carlos Ruiz Zafón

The Shadow of the Wind is a captivating novel set in post-war Barcelona. It tells the story of a young boy who discovers a mysterious book in a forgotten corner of the city’s library. As he delves into the world of the book, he becomes entwined in a web of intrigue, love, and danger. This novel is a beautiful exploration of the power of literature and the impact it can have on our lives.

2. The Book of Night Women by Marlon James

The Book of Night Women is a powerful historical novel set in 19th-century Jamaica. It tells the story of a group of enslaved women who band together to fight against their oppressors. This book is a harrowing and beautiful portrayal of the resilience and courage of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity.

3. The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao by Junot Díaz

This novel is a vibrant and energetic exploration of Dominican-American identity, told through the lens of a young man named Oscar Wao. The story is a rich tapestry of history, literature, and family, and it is a testament to the power of storytelling to connect us to the past and to one another.

4. The Road by Cormac McCarthy

The Road is a haunting and poignant novel about a father and his young son as they navigate a post-apocalyptic world. The book is a powerful exploration of the human spirit and the lengths to which we will go to protect those we love.

5. The Handmaid’s Tale by Margaret Atwood

The Handmaid’s Tale is a chilling dystopian novel set in a future America where women are subjugated and stripped of their rights. The story follows the life of a woman named Offred, who is forced to become a handmaid in a totalitarian regime. This book is a powerful commentary on the importance of freedom and the dangers of unchecked power.

6. The Time Traveler’s Wife by Audrey Niffenegger

The Time Traveler’s Wife is a unique and moving love story about a man who suffers from a genetic disorder that causes him to involuntarily time travel. The novel explores the impact of this disorder on his life, his relationships, and his marriage.

7. The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time by Mark Haddon

The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time is a heartwarming and insightful novel about a young man with autism who sets out to solve the mystery of a dead dog. The book is a powerful exploration of the human experience and the importance of understanding and accepting others.

8. The Namesake by Jhumpa Lahiri

The Namesake is a poignant and insightful novel about the challenges of growing up as a first-generation American. The story follows the life of Gogol, a young man struggling to reconcile his Indian heritage with his American identity.

9. The God of Small Things by Arundhati Roy

The God of Small Things is a powerful and haunting novel set in India. The story follows the lives of two siblings, Rahel and Esthappen, as they navigate the complexities of caste, class, and love in a rapidly changing society.

10. The Power of One by Bryce Courtenay

The Power of One is a sweeping epic set in South Africa during the early 20th century. The novel follows the life of a young boy named Peekay, who is forced to confront the harsh realities of apartheid and the power of the human spirit.

Conclusion:

These are just a few of the many incredible books that deserve your attention.
