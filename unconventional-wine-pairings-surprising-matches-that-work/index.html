Unconventional Wine Pairings: Surprising Matches That Work

4 min · 792 words

When it comes to wine pairings, most people stick to the classics - a robust red with steak, a crisp white with seafood, and perhaps a sweet dessert wine to finish. But what if I told you that there’s a whole world of unconventional wine pairings waiting to be explored? Surprising matches that work in ways you wouldn’t expect. In this blog post, we’re going to dive into the realm of unconventional wine pairings and discover some surprising combinations that will elevate your dining experience to new heights.

1. Popcorn and Champagne: It may sound strange, but the salty crunch of popcorn pairs surprisingly well with the effervescence of Champagne. The bubbles cleanse your palate between bites, allowing you to fully enjoy the flavors of both the popcorn and the wine. Next time you’re having a movie night, grab a bottle of Champagne and a bowl of buttery popcorn, and prepare to be amazed.

2. Sushi and Riesling: Sushi is often paired with sake, but if you’re looking for a unique twist, try it with a crisp Riesling. The natural sweetness of the wine complements the delicate flavors of the fish, while the acidity cuts through the richness of the soy sauce and wasabi. This unexpected pairing will take your sushi experience to a whole new level.

3. Barbecue and Zinfandel: Barbecue is all about bold flavors and smoky goodness, and what better wine to pair it with than a robust Zinfandel? The jammy fruit flavors of the wine complement the sweetness and spiciness of the barbecue sauce, while the tannins help to cut through the richness of the meat. Whether you’re grilling ribs, brisket, or pulled pork, reach for a bottle of Zinfandel to enhance the flavors of your barbecue feast.

4. Chocolate and Syrah: We all know that chocolate and red wine go hand in hand, but have you ever tried pairing it with a Syrah? The dark fruit and peppery spice of the wine beautifully complement the richness and bitterness of dark chocolate. This decadent pairing is perfect for a romantic evening or as a sophisticated dessert option.

5. Cheeseburger and Malbec: Forget the traditional burger and beer combo - try pairing your cheeseburger with a bold and juicy Malbec. The tannins in the wine help to cut through the fattiness of the meat, while the ripe fruit flavors elevate the flavors of the burger. This unexpected pairing will make your next burger night a memorable one.

6. Spicy Thai Curry and Gewürztraminer: Thai cuisine is known for its complex flavors and spiciness, and what better way to cool down your taste buds than with a glass of Gewürztraminer? The floral and aromatic notes of the wine balance out the heat of the curry, while the slight sweetness enhances the flavors of the dish. This unique pairing will take your Thai takeaway to a whole new level.

7. Grilled Shrimp and Verdejo: Grilled shrimp is a summertime favorite, and when paired with a crisp and refreshing Verdejo, it becomes a match made in culinary heaven. The citrus notes of the wine complement the sweetness of the shrimp, while the acidity cuts through the richness of any accompanying sauces or dips. This unexpected pairing will make your seafood barbecue a hit with your guests.

These are just a few examples of unconventional wine pairings that work wonders. The key is to experiment and trust your taste buds. Don’t be afraid to step outside the box and try something new. You never know, you might just discover your new favorite combination. So, the next time you’re planning a meal, think beyond the classics and let your imagination guide you to surprising matches that will elevate your dining experience to new heights. Cheers!
