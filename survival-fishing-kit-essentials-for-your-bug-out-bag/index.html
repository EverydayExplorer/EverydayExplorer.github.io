Survival Fishing Kit: Essentials for Your Bug Out Bag

5 min · 961 words

In a world where uncertainty and unforeseen circumstances can strike at any time, being prepared is key. As a survivalist, one of the most important skills you can have is the ability to fish for your own food. Having a survival fishing kit in your bug out bag is an essential part of your preparedness plan. In this blog post, we will discuss the essentials that you need to include in your survival fishing kit.

1. Fishing Line

The first item you need in your survival fishing kit is a strong and durable fishing line. Look for a high-quality line that is capable of withstanding the wear and tear of survival situations. Braided lines are often a good choice, as they are less likely to break or fray. Make sure to pack enough fishing line to last you for an extended period of time.

2. Hooks

A variety of hooks in different sizes is essential for catching different types of fish. Pack a range of hooks, including small ones for small fish and larger ones for bigger catches. Make sure to choose hooks that are sharp and have barbs to improve your chances of catching fish successfully.

3. Lures

Lures are a great addition to your survival fishing kit as they can attract fish and increase your chances of a successful catch. Pack a variety of lures that mimic the appearance and movement of different types of baitfish. This will increase your chances of attracting a wide range of fish to your line.

4. Bobbers

Bobbers are important for keeping track of where your line is in the water and alerting you when a fish bites. They also help to suspend your bait at a specific depth. Choose bobbers that are brightly colored and easily visible, so you can keep track of your line even in murky water.

5. Sinkers

Sinkers are used to weigh down your line and keep your bait at the desired depth. Different sizes of sinkers are necessary for fishing in different conditions. Choose sinkers that are easy to attach and detach from your line, so you can quickly adjust your fishing depth as needed.

6. Fishing Knife

A sharp fishing knife is a must-have tool in your survival fishing kit. It can be used for cutting fishing line, cleaning fish, and various other tasks. Look for a compact knife with a folding blade to save space in your bug out bag.

7. Fishing Pliers

Fishing pliers are another essential tool for your survival fishing kit. They can be used for removing hooks from fish, tightening knots, and cutting fishing line. Look for pliers that are highly durable and rust-resistant, as they will likely be exposed to water and harsh conditions.

8. Fishing Guide or Book

If you are not an experienced angler, a fishing guide or book can be a valuable resource for learning about different fishing techniques, local fish species, and the best times to fish. Choose a guide or book that is specific to the regions where you may find yourself in a survival situation.

9. Compact Fishing Rod

While not absolutely necessary, a compact fishing rod can greatly improve your chances of catching fish in survival situations. Look for a collapsible fishing rod that can easily fit in your bug out bag. This will allow you to cast your line further and reach deeper waters, increasing your chances of a successful catch.

10. Tackle Box

Lastly, you will need a sturdy and waterproof tackle box to store all your fishing essentials. Look for a tackle box with different compartments and dividers to keep your fishing gear organized and easily accessible.

Conclusion

A survival fishing kit is an essential part of your bug out bag. It provides you with the ability to catch fish for food when other food sources may be scarce. By including the essentials mentioned above, you can increase your chances of a successful catch in a survival situation. Remember to practice your fishing skills before an emergency arises, as proficiency is key to successful fishing. Stay prepared and stay safe!
